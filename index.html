Surfing!

When the waves are good, nobody works.

Being a typical Californian, I grew up in the water. There are few things more enjoyable to me than spending an early morning out on the waves. Surfing is a surreal experience regardless of the quality of the waves. The best way to describe feeling is Zen, I suppose. You are surrounded by all the natural life teeming in the ocean, moving with the sway of the sea, and syncing up with the power of a wave. It is quite difficult to put in to words what surfing is all about to those who have not experienced it, but I would encourage those who haven't to get out there and try it! As the Costa Ricans say Pura Vida.

Adventure Racing!

Adventure racing is a horse of another color when compared to something as mellow in nature as surfing. The above photo is actually of a friend of mine and I during one of these races. For a lack of a better word, the best description for this sport is intense. The nature of these events is to throw as many challenging obstacles at you as possible while you are traversing miles of uphill terrain. To finish one of these races takes quite a bit in the way of training, but it is a great way to motivate yourself to stay in shape!

Shooting Pool!

My father has been playing pool since long before I was born, so as I grew up he taught me a little of the game. Using the phrase the game is a little misleading. I actually enjoy playing quite a few games on a pool table, though I typically stick to either 9-ball or 8-ball. Pool, or billiards, is what my dad and I bonded over once I was old enough to play. This is a hobby that I have extended to my close group of friends and tried to teach them the basics as I know them which are as follows:

  • Get a good solid tripod stance
  • Get as low as possible on the ball over the stick
  • All movement should be from the elbow down
  • Hit the center of the ball with a followed-through stroke
